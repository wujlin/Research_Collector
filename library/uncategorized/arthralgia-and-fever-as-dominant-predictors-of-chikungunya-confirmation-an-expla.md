## Abstract

The clinical differentiation of Chikungunya from other acute febrile illnesses poses a significant diagnostic challenge due to the substantial symptom overlap among co-circulating arboviruses such as Dengue and Zika, particularly in resource-constrained settings where laboratory confirmation is frequently delayed or unavailable. This study proposes an interpretable machine learning framework for the prediction of laboratory-confirmed Chikungunya cases versus suspected cases that were subsequently discarded after laboratory investigation, using a large-scale, nationally representative dataset of 12,709 notification records derived from Brazil's Information System for Notifiable Diseases (SINAN), spanning 2013 to 2020 and encompassing 26 clinical symptom, comorbidity, and sociodemographic features. Four supervised learning algorithms—Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, and LightGBM—were systematically evaluated under 5-fold stratified cross-validation, with Random Forest achieving the highest discriminative performance (AUC = 0.785 ± 0.005) and XGBoost demonstrating the best probability calibration reliability.
