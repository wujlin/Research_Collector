## Abstract

In Brazil, one litre of tap water costs €0.00063. In Japan, €0.00186. In India (Mumbai), €0.000065. In Denmark, €0.00920. Across nineteen countries on five continents, the market price of potable water per litre is between approximately 444,000 and 38,500,000 times lower than the market price of residential space per square metre. Expressed as a percentage premium of space over water: between roughly 44 billion percent and 3.8 trillion percent, depending on the country and its water governance model. The first question of this paper is: are there other goods pairs like this anywhere — in economics, in biology, or in history? We searched systematically and could not find one that simultaneously satisfies four conditions: both goods are survival-relevant; both carry full market prices; the ratio exceeds 44 billion percent; and the more biologically vital good is the cheaper one. We propose this may be unique. Three structural paradoxes compound the strangeness.
