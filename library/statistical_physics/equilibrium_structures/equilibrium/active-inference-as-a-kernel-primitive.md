## Abstract

The Free Energy Principle (FEP) is typically applied to artificial systems as a training objective computed over discrete episodes—a loss function that shapes learning but does not govern real-time dynamics. We describe a different use: implementing the FEP as the kernel of the system tick loop, so that expected free energy directly determines the rate of ongoing dynamical integration. In this formulation, cognitive processing is not an algorithm that runs to completion and returns a result. It is a continuous-time physical process governed by a Langevin stochastic differential equation, whose integration step is modulated moment-to-moment by a compound arousal signal derived from five independent free energy estimators. Surprise accelerates the dynamics. Familiarity slows them. The dream cycle emerges naturally as a low- arousal regime in which consolidation dominates over perception. We provide the differential geometry of this integration via the Fokker-Planck equation and prove that the arousal-modulated Langevin dynamics preserve the free energy minimum as an attractor.
