## Abstract

This paper reconstructs aggregate intellectual mobility over roughly two millennia using birth and death locations of more than 150,000 notable individuals. It treats locations and mobility flows as a network and applies network science and complexity methods to identify long-term patterns in cultural centers, attraction, and historical change across Europe and North America.

## Key Contributions

- Turns cultural history into a measurable mobility network based on birth-to-death location data.
- Uses large-scale visualization and network statistics to identify cultural centers and their historical shifts.
- Connects quantitative network science with macro-historical narratives.
- Provides a reference case for using spatial mobility networks to study cultural, demographic, and urban history.
