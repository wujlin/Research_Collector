## Abstract

This Science Policy Forum argues that LLM-powered agentic swarms can coordinate influence operations at scale, fabricating consensus and adapting to social environments in ways that exceed traditional bots.

## Why It Matters

It is not a modeling paper in the narrow sense, but it shows the policy-risk endpoint of LLM population dynamics: autonomous agents can produce synthetic social proof and population-level manipulation.

## Connection

This paper marks the negative version of the same phenomenon we care about: population-level dynamics cannot be inferred from single-agent behavior alone.
