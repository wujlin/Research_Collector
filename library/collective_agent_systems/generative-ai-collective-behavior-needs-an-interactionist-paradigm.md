## Abstract

This perspective paper argues that LLM-based agents require an interactionist paradigm. Their collective behavior cannot be understood only from individual model capabilities or biases, because pretrained social priors and in-context adaptation interact with social context to generate emergent population-level phenomena.

## Why It Matters

This is the direct intersection of Leibo and Baronchelli. It gives the conceptual umbrella for reading multi-agent RL, social conventions, LLM populations, and generative ABM as one research program.

## Connection

For synthetic city research, this paper supports a shift from `single conditional generator` to `population-level generative dynamics under interaction and constraints`.
