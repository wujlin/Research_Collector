## Abstract

This paper argues that real social dilemmas are temporally extended rather than one-shot matrix games. Cooperation is therefore a property of policies, not isolated actions. The authors introduce sequential social dilemmas in Markov games and study learned behavior in Gathering and Wolfpack environments.

## Why It Matters

This is the foundational Leibo paper for your current interest. It moves cooperation/conflict from static game labels into spatial-temporal policy learning.

## Connection

For city route or population simulation, the key lesson is that collective behavior can depend on trajectories, resource abundance, and long-term feedback, not just instantaneous choice.
