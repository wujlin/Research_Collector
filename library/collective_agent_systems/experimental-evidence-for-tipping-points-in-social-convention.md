## Abstract

This Science article experimentally tests critical-mass theory for changing social conventions. In a controlled convention-formation system, a committed minority can overturn an established behavior once it reaches a critical size. The paper provides direct empirical evidence that tipping-point dynamics can occur in social convention change.

## Why It Matters

This is the natural second paper after the spontaneous-emergence paper. The first explains how a convention can form; this one asks how an established convention can be displaced.

## Connection

For synthetic city or route-choice dynamics, this paper gives a way to think about abrupt shifts in population-level modes: not as independent path changes, but as tipping of a convention-like collective state.
