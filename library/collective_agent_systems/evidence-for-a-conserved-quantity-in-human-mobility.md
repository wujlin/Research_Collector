## Abstract

This paper analyzes multi-year mobility traces and shows that individuals continuously explore new places while maintaining a stable-size set of familiar locations. The typical activity-set capacity is about 25 locations. The result reconciles exploration with the stable exploitation of a small set of repeatedly visited places.

## Why It Matters

This is the bridge from Baronchelli's social convention work into urban mobility. It says that mobility is not just flow volume or OD probability; it also has a conserved individual-level structure.

## Connection

For route and synthetic population research, this paper suggests looking for stable latent capacities or repeated sets, not only aggregate distributions.
