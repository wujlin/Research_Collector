## Abstract

This paper asks how shared conventions emerge in complex decentralized social systems. It reports web-based experiments showing that universal social conventions can arise from local coordination alone, without centralized institutions, global incentives, leadership, or aggregate information about the population. It also shows that network structure can direct the speed and trajectory of convention formation.

## Why It Matters

This is the cleanest starting point for Baronchelli's convention line. It turns `convention` into an empirical collective-dynamics object: a population-level regularity that emerges from repeated local interactions.

## Connection

For route generation, this suggests reading corridor modes not only as geometry, but as repeated population choices stabilized by local constraints and interaction structure.
