## Abstract

This paper asks whether populations of LLM agents can spontaneously form social conventions through decentralized local interactions. It reports that LLM populations can converge to conventions, develop collective biases that are not obvious at the individual-agent level, and be shifted by committed minority agents.

## Why It Matters

This is the direct bridge from Baronchelli's human convention experiments to LLM populations. It tests whether the same convention/tipping machinery reappears when the agents are generative AI systems.

## Connection

For our current research, this is a key paper for treating route corridors, generated preferences, or synthetic population modes as emergent population-level regularities rather than independent outputs.
